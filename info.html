HEART RATE

Heart rate, often abbreviated as HR, is a vital physiological parameter that
measures the number of times a person's heart beats per minute (bpm).
It is a crucial indicator of cardiovascular health and can vary depending on
factors such as age, fitness level, and overall health. Heart rate is typically
measured by counting the pulses at various pulse points (e.g., wrist,
neck,or chest) or by using electronic devices such as heart rate monitors

  • Resting Heart Rate (RHR):
      For infants (0-1 year): 100-160 BPM
       For children (1-10 years): 70-120 BPM
      For adults (above 10 years): 60-100 BPM
